# Writing Style: Executive Audiences

Use this style for: design reviews with leadership, roadmap presentations, milestone summaries, anything going to a VP or above.

---

## Structure

1. **Lead with the decision or "so what."** Executives skim. Put the answer first.
2. **Visuals before prose.** Link to the Figma file. Show the design. Then explain.
3. **Context in one sentence.** Why does this matter? What were we trying to solve?
4. **What we decided** and why.
5. **What's next** (and who owns it).

## Tone

- Confident and direct. No hedging.
- Strategic framing: tie design work to user outcomes and business goals.
- Respectful of their time. If it can be one paragraph, make it one paragraph.

## Numbers and Impact

- Anchor to metrics when possible: "This change addresses the 34% drop-off at step 3 of onboarding."
- Use comparisons: "This pattern is used by [Competitor A] and [Competitor B]. Ours differs in [specific way]."
- Be clear about what's measurable post-launch.

## What to Avoid

- Long rationale before the point
- Designer-speak ("visual hierarchy," "affordances," "gestalt") without plain-language explanation
- Asking for approval on things you should already be deciding
- Burying the ask at the end
